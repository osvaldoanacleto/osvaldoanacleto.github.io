I completed my PhD in Statistics in 2012, supervised by Catriona Queen and Paul Garthwaite at the Open University. You can find here an overview of the research I developed during my PhD.

I have a BSc. and a Master degree in Statistics, both obtained at the Federal University of São Carlos (UFSCar - Brazil). While I was studying for both degrees, I worked under the supervision of Francisco Louzada on non parametric survival analysis.

Prior to coming to the UK in 2009, I worked as a temporary lecturer for UFSCar. In this post, I was responsible for devising and delivering statistics courses to undergraduate students from several areas and also a statistical inference course for Statistics students. While at UFSCar, I was also involved in some projects from a statistical consulting company.

I have also worked from 2005 to 2009 for Banco Itaú (now Itaú Unibanco) applying and developing a variety of statistical models to large financial datasets of bank customers, as well as supervising data analysts and providing statistical support and advice in the Research and Development Credit area.
